How to play Fish

Fish is a team deduction game. Work out where the cards are, collect complete six-card sets with your teammates, and declare those sets before the other team does.

1. Cards and teams

  • 3v3: 54 cards and nine sets, including two jokers.
  • 2v2: 48 cards and eight sets; the 8s and jokers are removed.
  • Each suit has a low set (2–7) and a high set (9–Ace).
  • In 3v3, the ninth set is the four 8s plus the red and black jokers.

2. Asking for a card

  • On your turn, choose an opponent and ask for one exact card.
  • You may ask only from a set represented in your own hand.
  • You may not ask for a card you already hold.
  • If the opponent has it, they give it to you and you keep the turn.
  • If they do not, they take the next turn.

3. Use the clues

Every ask reveals information. A successful ask shows who had the card. A denial proves that player does not have it. Also track which sets players ask from and how many cards they have left.

4. Declaring a set

  • You may declare at any time, even during another player’s turn.
  • Name a set and assign all six cards to their exact holders on your team.
  • A correct declaration gives your team the set.
  • A wrong declaration gives the set to the opposing team.
  • Either way, all six cards leave play.

5. Winning

  • In 3v3, the first team to five sets wins.
  • In 2v2, the first team to four sets wins.
  • If you run out of cards, you spectate while the remaining players finish.

6. Simulator tools

  • Analyze move gives a short deduction-based hint.
  • Bot turns can run automatically or one move at a time.
  • After a game, you can view, copy, or download a full report.
About the bots: Bots use only their own cards and public information. Stronger difficulties make more complete deductions from denials, hand counts, previous asks, and teammate signals; they cannot inspect hidden hands.
